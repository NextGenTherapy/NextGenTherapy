# Positioning Brief — Next Gen Therapy

**Read this before writing any copy for the site.** This is the strategic reference. The individual prompts in this folder operationalise what's in here. If a prompt and this brief disagree, this brief wins.

---

## The one-sentence positioning

**Psychodynamic therapy in Colchester and online for women, neurodivergent adults, and teenagers — from a BACP-registered therapist who speaks English and Romanian and specialises in the quiet kind of burnout that looks like high-functioning from the outside.**

---

## The real problem the site is solving

Andreea is getting clients from Psychology Today and BACP directory, not from her own website. The website is a brochure people land on *after* they've found her elsewhere. It needs to become a discovery engine in its own right — specifically, it needs to rank for the searches her ideal clients actually make, and then convert them.

**Current state:** 12+ generic pages, no voice, no specificity, cannibalising each other in Google, no testimonials allowed, no clear niche, trying to speak to everyone.

**Target state:** ~10 deliberate pages organised around five real specialisms, in Andreea's actual voice, with concrete credentials replacing social proof, and a long tail of location pages + evergreen blog posts pulling in targeted organic traffic.

**Business target:** move from 12 client hours/week to 20–22. Roughly 75% growth. 6–9 month horizon. This needs ~8–10 additional first enquiries per month.

---

## The five real specialisms (in priority order)

1. **Women — especially high-functioning, hyper-aware women** with burnout, overthinking, self-esteem, body image, anxiety, avoidance. This is where Andreea gets her best outcomes. The site's primary voice and primary traffic target.
2. **Adult neurodiversity** — ADHD and autism, affirmative practice, sensory-aware room.
3. **Teenagers** with mild self-harm, challenging behaviour, school anxiety. Parent-informed work.
4. **Romanian-speaking clients** — adults and young adults, bicultural identity, immigrant experience.
5. **Children 4–12** — play-based psychodynamic work with parent involvement. Real but secondary.

LGBTQ+ work is a layer inside #1 (specifically women in same-sex relationships), not a separate niche.

Everything outside these five is being demoted or cut: no couples, no over-40s, no CBT, no short-term, no forensic, no active addiction.

## The phrases that should drive the site

These are the actual phrases Andreea hears in first sessions. Use them verbatim as headlines, subheadings, meta descriptions, and blog post hooks. They are the single most valuable content asset in this project.

- "I can't stop overthinking."
- "I'm avoiding situations because of my body image."
- "I feel stuck and burnt out."
- "I fit everything in and somehow still feel like I'm slipping something."
- "I can't seem to stop worrying."
- "I don't know who I am anymore."
- "I feel like I'm failing at everything."
- "I can't switch off."
- "I look fine and I'm not fine."

The current homepage hero says *"You don't have to keep holding it together alone."* That's decent. The phrases above are better. The new hero should use one of them.

---

## Credibility replaces testimonials

BACP prohibits most testimonial use and Andreea isn't comfortable with them anyway. Compensate with **concrete, verifiable, named** credentials:

- **MA Psychodynamic Psychotherapy, University of Essex, 2020**
- **BACP Registered Member** (linked to her BACP profile)
- NHS Essex — workshops in primary and secondary schools
- Sir Bobby Robson School (Ipswich) — SEN provision
- Mind, Mid & North East Essex — youth work
- YMCA Ipswich — current project (primary school + residential support)
- Post-qualification CPD: neurodiversity, eating disorders, trauma, attachment, LGBTQ+, addiction
- Ongoing clinical supervision + personal therapy

None of this appears clearly on the current About page. Putting it there replaces the trust-building work a testimonial would do. Named organisations are more credible than anonymous quotes anyway.

## Voice cheatsheet

Write as Andreea. First-person, warm, direct, calm. Specific. Short paragraphs. Honest about limits.

### Ban list (never appears on the site)
- "Safe, nurturing space"
- "Empowering"
- "Journey"
- "Unlock your potential"
- "Holistic wellness"
- "Transformative"
- "Ready to take the first step?"
- "Compassionate support for..."
- Any sentence that could apply to any therapist in the UK

### Good-sounding examples to calibrate to
- *"I've been providing psychodynamic therapy in Colchester for over six years. Before that I trained at the University of Essex and worked in NHS schools, SEN provision, and with Mind and YMCA. The work I do now is a continuation of that — slower, more considered, and one person at a time."*
- *"I don't offer short-term or CBT-style work. If you want six sessions of tools and worksheets, I'm not the right person. If you want to understand why the same pattern keeps showing up in your life, that's what I do."*
- *"The room is ground floor, quiet, and sensory-aware. Stimming is welcome. There are fidgets on the desk if you want them. Lighting is soft. I'm not going to ask you to sit still."*

---

## The "Is this right for you?" principle

Andreea loses most lost-leads to people who wanted CBT or short-term work. Rather than trying to convert them, the site should **qualify them out early** with a dedicated page that says, plainly:

> "If you want a toolkit and six sessions, here are three good Colchester therapists who do that well. If you want to understand why this keeps happening, keep reading."

This is counterintuitive but it works. It builds trust with the right clients, and the wrong ones leave without wasting a 15-min call slot. It also ranks well because it's genuinely useful and nobody else writes pages like this.

## SEO strategy summary

**Target 1 — Local:** 7–8 location landing pages for towns in her catchment (Wivenhoe, Mersea, Tiptree, Marks Tey, Manningtree, Clacton, Ipswich, Colchester proper). Each lightweight, each targeting "therapist [town]" queries.

**Target 2 — Niche:** 
- Romanian-speaking therapy (near-empty niche)
- Adult ADHD / autism affirmative therapy
- Women's burnout / high-achieving women anxiety

**Target 3 — Evergreen content:** 10 cornerstone blog posts, each 1500–2500 words, each answering a specific search query from a persona. Published over 3 months then largely left alone to rank.

**Target 4 — Technical:** Schema markup, meta titles/descriptions, internal linking, sitemap, GBP optimisation.

**Not the target:** weekly blog treadmill, aggressive social media, paid ads, cold outreach.
