# Blog post brief: Neurodivergent burnout: when masking costs more than you can afford

**Slug:** /blog/neurodivergent-burnout-masking
**Target query:** autistic burnout
**Secondary queries:** neurodivergent burnout, adhd burnout, masking exhaustion
**Cornerstone link:** /neurodiversity
**Target length:** 1,500–2,500 words
**Persona:** The Late-Diagnosed Neurodivergent Adult

## Angle

This post speaks directly to neurodivergent adults (particularly women) who have spent years masking and are now hitting a wall. Andreea writes as an affirmative therapist who understands that burnout isn't a personal failing—it's the predictable result of trying to function in a neurotypical world without support. The post validates the reader's exhaustion while offering psychodynamic therapy as a space to unmask, grieve, and rebuild.

## Structure

1. **Opening hook** — The moment when you can't do it anymore. When the strategies stop working.
2. **What neurodivergent burnout looks like** — Different from regular burnout. Loss of skills, increased sensory sensitivity, inability to mask anymore.
3. **The cost of masking** — What masking actually requires: constant monitoring, suppressing needs, performing "normal"
4. **Why it happens when it does** — Life transitions, additional stressors, post-diagnosis identity shifts, accumulation over years
5. **The grief underneath** — Mourning the energy spent, the relationships strained, the self hidden
6. **Recovery isn't about masking better** — It's about needing to mask less. Building a life that fits your brain.
7. **What affirmative therapy offers** — A space where you don't have to mask. Stimming welcomed. Sensory needs respected.
8. **The room** — Soft lighting, fidgets available, no eye contact requirements, stimming welcomed
9. **Closing** — Invitation to explore neurodiversity page or book a call
