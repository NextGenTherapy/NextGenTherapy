## Angle

This post demystifies the first therapy session for people who are nervous about starting. Andreea writes with warmth and honesty, describing exactly what happens—from the free consultation call to the actual first session. The post addresses common fears (What if I cry? What if I have nothing to say? What if I don't like the therapist?) and positions the first session as a "vibe check" rather than an assessment.

## Structure

1. **Opening hook** — Acknowledge the nerves. Most people are anxious before their first session.
2. **Before session one: the free 15-minute call** — What it is (logistics, questions, vibe check) and what it isn't (not therapy)
3. **The room** — What to expect physically: ground floor, quiet, plants, comfortable seating. Sensory considerations.
4. **What actually happens in session one** — "Tell me what brought you here." Getting to know each other. Comfort before assessment.
5. **Common fears addressed** — What if I cry? (That's fine.) What if I have nothing to say? (We'll find our way.) What if I don't like you? (Then I'm not the right therapist, and that's okay.)
6. **There's no right way to do it** — No script, no perfect way to be a client
7. **After the first session** — What happens next. Deciding whether to continue. Weekly, open-ended structure.
8. **Andreea's approach** — Real quote: "The first session is really a vibe check. Before anything else, you need to feel comfortable enough with me to want to come back."
9. **Closing** — Invitation to book a free 15-minute call
