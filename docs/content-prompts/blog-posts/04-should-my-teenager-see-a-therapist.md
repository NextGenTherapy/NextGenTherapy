# Blog post brief: Should my teenager see a therapist? A parent's honest guide

**Persona:** The Worried Parent

## Angle

This post speaks directly to parents (usually mothers) who are lying awake at night wondering if their teenager needs professional help. Andreea writes with warmth and honesty, acknowledging that parents often feel guilty, uncertain, or worried they're overreacting. The post helps parents recognise signs that therapy might help while being clear about what teen therapy actually involves—including confidentiality, parental involvement, and what happens in the room.

## Structure

1. **Opening hook** — Acknowledge the 2am worry: "Something's changed and I don't know what to do."
2. **Signs that therapy might help** — Withdrawal, mood changes, school refusal, self-harm, anxiety, anger. Changes that persist.
3. **Signs it might be normal teenage development** — Some withdrawal is developmentally appropriate. How to tell the difference.
4. **The CAMHS waitlist reality** — 12+ months in many areas. Private therapy as a bridge or alternative.
5. **What teen therapy actually looks like** — Not lying on a couch. Talking, creative work, building trust.
6. **Confidentiality: what parents need to know** — Teens need privacy to open up. Parents get updates without details. This is a feature, not a bug.
7. **Your role as a parent** — You're not failing by seeking help. You're noticing something and responding.
8. **In-person vs online for teens** — Why online is 16+ only (safeguarding, privacy concerns with younger teens)
9. **Closing** — Invitation to explore teen therapy page or book a 15-minute call to discuss your specific situation
