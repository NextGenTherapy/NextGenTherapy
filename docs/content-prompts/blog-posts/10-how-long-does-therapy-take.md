## Angle

This post answers one of the most common therapy questions with honesty rather than evasion. Andreea explains why psychodynamic therapy is open-ended, what that actually means, and who it's for. The post positions open-ended work as a feature, not a bug—while being clear that it's not the right approach for everyone.

## Structure

1. **Opening hook** — The honest answer: it depends. But that's not a cop-out.
2. **Why people ask this question** — Wanting to budget time/money, wanting to know when they'll feel better, anxiety about commitment
3. **What "open-ended" actually means** — Not forever. Not aimless. However long it takes to do the work.
4. **The case for not rushing** — Some patterns took decades to form. They don't shift in 6 weeks.
5. **What affects how long therapy takes** — What you're working on, your history, how often you come, what's happening in your life
6. **The structure: weekly sessions** — Why weekly matters for psychodynamic work. Building the relationship.
7. **How you'll know when you're done** — Not a graduation ceremony. A felt sense that you've got what you came for.
8. **Who open-ended therapy isn't for** — People who want a specific skill, a quick fix, or a structured programme
9. **The practical side: cost and commitment** — £60/session, weekly. Being honest about the investment.
10. **Closing** — Invitation to explore /is-this-right-for-you or book a call
