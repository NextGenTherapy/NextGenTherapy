# Blog post brief: High-functioning anxiety: what it actually looks like

**Slug:** /blog/high-functioning-anxiety-women

**Cornerstone link:** /therapy-for-women

**Persona:** The High-Functioning Woman

## Angle

This post speaks directly to the woman who has never called herself anxious because she's too busy succeeding. Andreea writes from experience of working with high-achieving women who look fine to everyone else but are exhausted, hyper-vigilant, and can't switch off. The post validates the reader's experience while gently introducing the idea that psychodynamic therapy explores *why* this pattern keeps repeating—not just how to manage symptoms.

## Structure

1. **Opening hook** — Use a real client phrase: *"I can't stop overthinking."* or *"I fit everything in and somehow still feel like I'm slipping something."*
2. **What high-functioning anxiety actually looks like** — The invisible signs: over-preparation, difficulty saying no, Sunday-night dread, physical symptoms dismissed as stress
3. **Why it's easy to miss** — You're praised for being reliable, productive, on top of things. The cost is hidden.
4. **The problem with just "coping better"** — CBT worksheets and breathing apps address symptoms, not roots
5. **What psychodynamic therapy offers instead** — Understanding the *why*. Where did this pattern start? What are you really afraid of?
6. **A note on burnout** — The connection between chronic high-functioning anxiety and eventual burnout
7. **Closing** — Invitation to explore further, link to book a 15-minute call
