## Angle

This post addresses a common question from exhausted, high-achieving women: is this burnout, depression, or both? Andreea writes from clinical experience, helping readers understand the overlap and differences without offering a diagnosis. The post positions psychodynamic therapy as a way to understand the underlying patterns—not just manage symptoms—and gently challenges the "just push through" mentality that often precedes both conditions.

## Structure

1. **Opening hook** — Use a real client phrase: *"I feel stuck and burnt out."* or *"I can't switch off."*
2. **Why this question matters** — Both feel awful. Treatment approaches can differ. And sometimes it's both.
3. **What burnout looks like** — Exhaustion tied to specific demands, cynicism, reduced effectiveness. Often still functional but running on empty.
4. **What depression looks like** — Pervasive low mood, loss of interest, physical symptoms. Not tied to one area of life.
5. **Where they overlap** — The danger zone: chronic burnout can slide into depression. High-functioning depression masks as burnout.
6. **Why "self-care" isn't enough** — Bubble baths don't fix structural problems or deep-rooted patterns
7. **What psychodynamic therapy offers** — Understanding *why* you pushed yourself to this point. What are you really afraid of? What would happen if you stopped?
8. **A note on rest** — Rest is necessary but not sufficient. Understanding is what changes patterns.
9. **Closing** — Invitation to explore therapy for women page or book a call
