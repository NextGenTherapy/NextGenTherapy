# Blog post brief: When body image and eating feel like the problem

**Persona:** The High-Functioning Woman

## Angle

This post speaks to women who struggle with food and body image but may not fit (or identify with) a clinical eating disorder diagnosis. Andreea writes from experience working with women whose relationship with food and their body is complicated—often intertwined with control, self-worth, and identity. The post validates the reader's experience while gently suggesting that the "problem" isn't really about food.

## Structure

1. **Opening hook** — Use real client phrase: *"I'm avoiding situations because of my body image."*
2. **When eating becomes the focus** — Restriction, bingeing, obsession with "healthy eating," exercise as punishment. The forms it takes.
3. **Why it's not really about food** — Food and body become the arena where other struggles play out: control, self-worth, coping with emotions.
4. **The problem with "just eat normally"** — Why well-meaning advice misses the point
5. **What therapy offers** — A space to understand what's underneath. What are you really hungry for? What are you trying to control?
6. **Working psychodynamically with eating difficulties** — Not meal plans or food diaries. Understanding patterns and their roots.
7. **The connection to other struggles** — Anxiety, perfectionism, relationship difficulties. Often intertwined.
8. **A note on when more support is needed** — When eating difficulties require specialist or medical support alongside therapy
9. **Closing** — Invitation to explore therapy for women page or book a call
