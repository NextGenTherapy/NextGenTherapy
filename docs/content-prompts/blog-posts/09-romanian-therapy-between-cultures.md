## Angle

This post speaks directly to Romanian speakers in the UK who are looking for a therapist who understands their experience—not just linguistically, but culturally. Andreea writes from her own position as a Romanian-born therapist, acknowledging the specific challenges of bicultural identity, intergenerational differences, and the emotional texture that only comes through in your first language. The post is primarily in English with some Romanian phrases woven in naturally.

## Structure

1. **Opening hook** — The moment when you realise you need to explain your whole cultural context before you can explain your feelings
2. **What gets lost in translation** — Emotional texture, cultural assumptions, the weight of certain phrases that don't translate
3. **The immigrant experience, unnamed** — Guilt about leaving, pressure to succeed, complicated relationships with home. Specific to Romanian but resonant.
4. **Parenting between cultures** — Raising children who are both Romanian and British. The gaps in understanding.
5. **Why language matters in therapy** — Some things can only be said in Romanian. The relief of not translating yourself.
6. **Second-generation experience** — For those who grew up here with Romanian parents: the in-between identity
